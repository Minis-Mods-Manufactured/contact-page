Frequently Asked Questions

What financing options are available?

We work with trusted lenders who specialize in manufactured home construction loans. Even if your credit isn’t perfect, we may still have a solution—most of our buyers do NOT have perfect credit.

  • FHA (Federal Housing Administration)
    Down Payment: As low as 3.5%
    Great for first-time homebuyers or those with limited savings.
  • VA (Veterans Affairs)
    Down Payment: 0%
    Available to veterans, active-duty military, and qualifying spouses.
  • USDA (U.S. Department of Agriculture)
    Down Payment: 0% (for eligible rural areas)
    Helps buyers purchase in rural areas with low or moderate income.
  • Conventional Loans
    Down Payment: Typically 5%–20%
    Offers more flexibility and competitive long-term terms.
What types of home loans are available?
  • Chattel Loan (Home-Only)
    Down Payment: Typically 5%–10%
    For the home only—not the land. Ideal for leased land, family land, or land you already own.
  • Land/Home Package Loan
    Down Payment: Varies
    Combines the home, land, and site work into one bundled loan with long-term financing.
  • Land-in-Lieu
    Down Payment: 0% (if land equity qualifies)
    Uses land you already own instead of a cash down payment. Land must meet lender requirements.

Yes, we do! While we don’t have homes already set up on land, we help you build a custom package that includes the home, land, and all necessary site work. How much money do I need to get started?

It's free to apply! Once we get your approval back from the bank, the dealer will require a $1000 deposit to hold a home/order one. Down payment requirements will vary and depend on your credit and lender program. We’ll guide you through your options!

Will applying for financing hurt my credit?

Not the way you might think! When you’re shopping for a mortgage, credit bureaus expect you to compare lenders. All mortgage credit checks done within a 45-day window count as just one inquiry on your credit report.

The score impact is usually minimal — often just a few points — and the long-term savings from getting a better rate far outweigh that dip. Knowing your credit position early also helps us match you with the right lenders and programs from the start.

“I don’t want to pull my credit yet” is a common worry, but applying gives you real buying power and clarity — without locking you into anything.
